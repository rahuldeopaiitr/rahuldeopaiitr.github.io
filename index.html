§ 01 — About

About

I am a PhD Research Scholar in the Department of Water Resources Development and Management at IIT Roorkee, working at the intersection of hydrology, flood hydrodynamics, water quality, and public-health risk. My research addresses a question that conventional flood assessments often overlook: not only where floodwater spreads, but also what contaminants it carries, how people are exposed, and what health risks may result.

My doctoral research focuses on quantifying public-health risks associated with contaminated floodwaters in flood-prone metropolitan regions. It examines the processes through which flooding mobilizes and transports microbial contamination and translates these dynamics into spatially explicit estimates of human exposure and infection risk under present and future climatic conditions.

As part of this research, I developed HyEco, a hybrid hydrodynamic–ecological modelling framework that integrates flood inundation modelling using MIKE+ and MIKE FLOOD, microbial fate-and-transport simulation using MIKE ECO Lab, and Quantitative Microbial Risk Assessment. The framework provides a systematic means of linking flood behaviour, contaminant transport, and population exposure to support health-risk assessment and informed urban flood management.

Beyond flood-related health risks, my research also examines hydroclimatic extremes, including design-rainfall regionalisation for data-scarce catchments, statistical downscaling, and satellite-based hydroclimatic assessment. I develop customised analytical workflows in Python and R and am interested in interdisciplinary collaborations on urban flood risk, water quality, climate adaptation, and environmental-health linkages.

§ 02 — Research

Research Themes

Theme 01

Hydro-Epidemiological Coupling

The HyEco framework — an end-to-end chain linking flood hydrodynamics to flood-borne infection risk, so a rainfall event can be traced to a human health outcome.

Theme 02

Urban Flood Hazard & Inundation

Hydrodynamic modelling of urban flooding in densely populated megacities, including spatio-temporal and geostatistical rainfall analysis for flood management.

Theme 03

Water Quality & QMRA

Modelling contaminant and pathogen fate in floodwater and translating exposure into infection probability through Quantitative Microbial Risk Assessment.

Theme 04

Climate & Socio-Economic Futures

Quantifying how urbanisation, climate change and socio-economic dynamics reshape flood and health risk, integrating regional climate models into flood modelling.
